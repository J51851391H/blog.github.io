A

Acquittal: A jury verdict that a criminal defendant is not guilty, or the finding of a judge that the evidence is insufficient to support a conviction.

Example: In a criminal trial, the defendant wins. She has been "acquitted" of the charges brought against her. She goes home instead of going to prison.

B

Burden of Proof: The obligation to present evidence to support one's claim or defense in a legal proceeding. Common examples include: preponderance of the evidence, clear and convincing evidence, manifest weight of the evidence, and beyond a reasonable doubt.

Example 1: Car accident. Injured person sues, to win she must convince the jury by a preponderance of the evidence (i.e. it's more than 50% likely) that the other driver is at fault.

Example 2: Murder. Sally is the prime suspect. To win, the prosecution must convince the jury "beyond a reasonable doubt" (only an unreasonable person would question) that the defendant is guilty.
